## 1. Business Problem Sumble Solves For SolarWinds

**Anchor to public goal.** Last public SolarWinds metrics (FY2024 release, March 2025): **Total ARR $729M (+7% YoY), Subscription ARR $311.7M (+34% YoY).** The gap is the entire game — SolarWinds is not adding meaningful net-new ARR, they're converting their installed base from perpetual to subscription. Every defection to Splunk / Datadog / Dynatrace is direct ARR loss with no offset.

**Under PE ownership, retention IS the strategy.** Turn/River Capital took SolarWinds private in February 2025 for $4.4B ($18.50/share). The PE thesis on a heavily-levered IT-monitoring legacy is cash extraction + cross-sell, not aggressive net-new. **#1 KPI: gross retention rate. #2 KPI: subscription conversion velocity.** Quarterly scoreboard.

**The problem with their current GTM stack.** SolarWinds' GTM tooling — caveat: numbers below partly conflated with N-able — is built for net-new outbound, not customer-base retention:

**Sumble's wedge:** customer-base early-warning system. Sumble's data — 543 Splunk teams, 449 Grafana teams, 112 Dynatrace job posts, 41 Datadog jobs at Leidos — is the *signal layer* the rest of their stack cannot produce.

## 3. Why Leidos Fits the Trophy Pattern

SolarWinds' commercial customer base has been bleeding to Datadog/Splunk/Dynatrace since 2020. **The federal contractor base has been their last sticky segment** — security clearances, FedRAMP compliance lock-in, and ATO migration costs make defection slower and more expensive.

But the lock-in is breaking. The FedRAMP marketplace now has Splunk Cloud (high), Splunk on-prem with FedRAMP-aligned configs, and Elastic Cloud as authorized alternatives. The decade-old SolarWinds entrenchment is now a *liability* rather than a moat.

Leidos is the ideal trophy pattern:
- **Largest fed contractor by SolarWinds team count (167)** — top of the customer pyramid
- **14 federal-contractor lookalikes** also in the install base (General Dynamics, Northrop Grumman, Peraton, SAIC, Raytheon, KBR, ManTech, HII, CACI, Jacobs — almost all VA/MD-based defense primes)
- SolarWinds already has a dedicated **"FED Renewals Conversion Sales" team in Reston, VA** (active job post 2026-04-30) — they are already focused on this segment; the playbook they need is the *signal layer*, not a strategy pivot
- Leidos has been a SolarWinds customer for years → relationship is non-cold; this is win-back with active-customer status, not pure outbound
