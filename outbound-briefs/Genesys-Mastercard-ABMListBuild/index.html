    Outbound Strategic Research Brief — Sumble → Genesys¶

    Date: 2026-05-06 Target (whom Sumble is pitching): Genesys (genesys.com) Anchor prospect (whom Genesys should pursue): Mastercard [NEW LOGO] Recommended recipient at Genesys: Veronica Servantez — SVP Product Marketing (multi-thread Erika Chandler, SVP Global RevOps + Larry Shurtz, CSO)

    TL;DR¶

    Sumble surfaces a $3M–$7M ACV new-logo deal at Mastercard invisible to ZoomInfo / Apollo / 6Sense / UserGems / Demandbase: Mastercard runs Avaya across 13 teams with 22 active jobs and 30 contact-center operators (last job posted 2026-01-18) — a textbook mid-migration F500 financial-services estate matching Genesys's named Visa / BNP Paribas / HSBC trophy pattern. 60–100 F500 lookalikes per year on Avaya/NICE migrations = ~$160M qualifying pipeline / quarter, $160M annualized new ARR at 25% win rate — about 18% of Genesys's $910M annual net-new Cloud ARR target from a single play, pre-IPO.

    1. Business Problem Sumble Solves For Genesys¶

    Genesys disclosed $2.6B Cloud ARR growing 35% YoY and AI ARR over $250M growing ~2× the platform, with a confidential IPO filing already in (mid-2025) and a $1.5B strategic investment from Salesforce + ServiceNow. 35% on $2.6B = ~$910M net-new Cloud ARR for the next year; at 4× pipeline coverage and 25% win rate, that is ~$3.6B of qualifying pipeline to materialize into the IPO narrative. The pre-IPO marketing reorg under Veronica Servantez (SVP Product Marketing, Apr 2025) and the new Demand Hub being stood up under VP Integrated Marketing Americas exist precisely to deliver this number.

    Their existing tools tell them which F500 has visited the AI page and an account intent score. Sumble produces it; the existing stack cannot.

    2. Anchor Prospect: Mastercard [NEW LOGO]¶

    Mastercard — global payments network, ~42,133 employees, ~$30B revenue. Diversified F500 financial-services giant with global contact-center operations supporting consumer, B2B, and issuer services. Direct trophy parallel to Genesys's named Visa relationship.

    Anchor type rationale: NEW LOGO. Mastercard runs Avaya (Genesys's primary legacy-displacement target); zero current Genesys deployment per Sumble enrichment.

    3. Why Mastercard Fits the Trophy Pattern¶

    Genesys's named trophies are F500 enterprise across BFSI / Telco / Hospitality / Travel: HSBC, Vodafone, Visa, Best Buy Canada, BNP Paribas, Virgin Atlantic. Mastercard is the direct fintech parallel to Visa — same global F500 payment-network shape, same multi-region contact-center structure, same regulatory + compliance pressure profile.

    The inflection point: Mastercard's Avaya footprint is mid-migration (22 active jobs across 13 teams as of January 2026). F500 contact-center modernization typically spans 24–36 months from "we are evaluating" to "we have signed a multi-year cloud contract." Mastercard is in the active-evaluation window, and Genesys's PE-to-IPO narrative needs F500 logos like this one signed before the listing.

    4. Financial Frame (Segment-by-Segment)¶

    Anchor deal at Mastercard¶

    • 30 Avaya-tagged contact-center operators surfaced + 22 active Avaya jobs across 13 teams → ~2,500–4,500 contact-center seats globally (estimating multipliers for non-tagged operators across global ops centers)
    • Genesys Cloud + Engage + Experience Orchestration enterprise pricing typically $1,500–2,500 per seat per year on bundled subscription
    • Estimated annual seat math: 2,500–4,500 seats × $1,500–2,500 = $3.75M–$11.25M
    • Estimated anchor ACV: $3M–$7M (recurring, lands on a multi-region phased deployment, expansion to AI agents + WEM in years 2–3)

    Net-new lookalike TAM for the "F500 Avaya/NICE migration" play¶

    • ~60–100 F500 enterprises in BFSI / Telecom / Hospitality / Insurance per year run Avaya OR NICE CXone with 10K+ employees AND have an active CCaaS/Conversational AI hiring signal (estimated from Sumble query base of ~118 US-HQ F500 Avaya/NICE estates in those verticals)
    • Avg ACV at this segment: $1.5M–$3M
    • 80 lookalike accounts × $2M avg = $160M qualifying pipeline per year
    • 25% win rate = $40M new ARR / year, ramping to $160M annual run-rate over 4-year contract durations

    → A single Sumble-surfaced play produces ~$40M–$160M of new Cloud ARR — roughly 4–18% of Genesys's $910M annual net-new Cloud ARR target before counting expansion plays into existing trophy accounts and pre-IPO logo-quality wins (which carry disproportionate IPO-narrative value).

    5. Strategic Frame — Why This Account, Why Now¶

    Trigger 1 (the bullseye): Mastercard's Avaya footprint is active mid-migration¶

    22 active Avaya jobs across 13 teams (last job posted 2026-01-18, 30 Avaya-tagged operators). That is the canonical "we are still hiring on the legacy platform but actively evaluating a cloud successor" pattern. F500 financial-services migrations off Avaya typically land in the 18–30-month buying window once active evaluation begins.

    Trigger 2: Direct trophy parallel to Visa (existing Genesys customer)¶

    Customer Genesys role Industry
    Visa Existing trophy F500 payments network
    BNP Paribas Existing trophy F500 European banking
    HSBC Existing trophy F500 global banking
    Mastercard Anchor target F500 payments network — direct Visa parallel

    The proof points to bring into the first conversation are already in Genesys's deck. Mastercard's procurement and CIO orgs will recognize the Visa case study by name.

    Trigger 3: Genesys's pre-IPO marketing rebuild + Salesforce/ServiceNow strategic investment¶

    David Norrie (SVP PMM EMEA, Aug 2024) + Veronica Servantez (SVP PMM, Apr 2025) reorganizing PMM. Demand Hub being stood up under VP Integrated Marketing Americas. The $1.5B Salesforce + ServiceNow co-investment unlocks deep co-sell motion. F500 logos like Mastercard are also Salesforce + ServiceNow customers — co-sell amplifier.

    Why the window will close¶

    F500 Avaya migrations resolve within 18–30 months of active evaluation. Once Mastercard issues the cloud-CCaaS RFP and downselects, the window closes for 5+ years. Pre-IPO timing pressure on Genesys means landing this within 2–3 quarters or losing IPO-narrative value.

    🥈 VP / SVP Customer Experience or Member Services (likely champion since Avaya consolidation runs through their P&L).

    7. What Sumble Surfaced (That ZoomInfo / Apollo / 6Sense / UserGems / Demandbase Cannot)¶

    • The team-level Avaya migration footprint. ZoomInfo and 6Sense show "Mastercard uses Avaya" — a single boolean. Sumble shows: 13 specific teams running Avaya, 22 active jobs across them, 30 named contact-center operators tagged, last job posted Jan 2026. That team-level granularity is the migration-phase signal. Firmographic tools cannot produce it because they treat tech adoption at company level, not team level.
    • The trophy-pattern fingerprint match. 6Sense and Demandbase show category intent. Sumble compares Mastercard's full deployment fingerprint (F500 financial-services + 42K employees + 13-team Avaya estate + 30 CC operators) against Genesys's named trophy roster (Visa, BNP Paribas, HSBC) and ranks structural match before any rep opens the account.
    • The timing layer for migration-buying-windows. UserGems flags job changes; it does not tell you a 22-job Avaya estate has been active for 60+ days post-prior-decision-cycle. Sumble's last-job-on-tech timestamps drive the migration-phase math.
    • Co-sell signal layering. Demandbase shows "Mastercard is in market." Sumble flags that Mastercard is also a Salesforce + ServiceNow customer at scale — which is the co-sell amplifier given Genesys's strategic investment partners. None of the existing stack joins those two facts into a play hypothesis.

    6. SearchTechnologies('genesys') + SearchTechnologies('nice cxone') — confirmed competitor slugs nice-cxone, avaya
    7.
