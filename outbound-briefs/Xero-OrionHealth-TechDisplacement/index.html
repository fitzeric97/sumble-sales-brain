    Xero NZ Mid-Market — Outbound Use Case Brief¶

    For: Rob Borland (Director of Sales, NZ — Xero) From: Eric Fitzgerald (Account Executive — Sumble) Built: 2026-05-06 Lead anchor: Orion Health [NEW LOGO]

    TL;DR¶

    NZ mid-market is where Xero's FY28 "more than double FY25 revenue" target gets built. Orion Health (Auckland, ~500 emp, healthcare SaaS) is sitting on a textbook displacement window: 24 NetSuite jobs open in 2026, post-HEALWELL-AI acquisition integration in motion, near-zero existing Xero footprint. Sumble found the trigger ZoomInfo can't see; replicate the play across 7 NZ mid-market lookalikes for ~$2–4M annualized new-logo TAM.

    1. Business problem Sumble solves for Xero NZ¶

    Sukhinder Singh Cassidy publicly committed to doubling FY25 revenue by FY28 and quadrupling TAM from NZD 750B → NZD 3.3T via AI. ANZ ARPU +12% YoY confirms mid-market is the lever — micro is saturated.

    Rob inherited the NZ direct seat in Feb 2026 (3 months in). His mandate: convert Xero's mid-market push into NZ pipeline.

    The gap in Xero's stack: - Salesforce: 335 jobs / 168 people / 107 teams (huge execution layer) - Salesforce Marketing Cloud: 118 jobs / 36 teams (heavy ABM/nurture investment) - Outreach: 3 jobs, Gong: 1 job / 4 teams - ZoomInfo: 1 person, 0 jobs. Clay, 6sense, Common Room, HG Insights, Demandbase: zero footprint.

    Translation: Xero is running mid-market plays out of Salesforce with no project-trigger or org-graph signal layer feeding the funnel. Rob's reps don't have a way to find the moment a target's finance stack opens. That's what Sumble surfaces.

    3. Why Orion Health fits Xero's NZ trophy pattern¶

    • Mid-market sweet spot. ~500 employees — bigger than micro, smaller than enterprise ERP. Xero's NZ mid-market product lands here cleanly.
    • NZ-HQ, software-savvy. Auckland-grown SaaS company. They understand cloud-native tooling and will evaluate on TCO + integration speed, not procurement inertia.
    • Post-acquisition decision window. HEALWELL acquired them in 2025. Post-M&A is the #1 trigger for system-of-record consolidation. The window is open now and will close once parent integration locks in.
    • Active 2026 NetSuite hiring proves they're spending on finance ops — not pausing, not cost-cutting. Budget is allocated; the question is whether it stays with NetSuite.

    4. Financial frame (segment-by-segment)¶

    Orion Health alone (the anchor): - NetSuite at ~500 emp scale in ANZ ≈ NZD 200–400K/yr list (mid-market ERP pricing). - Xero displacement opportunity = core financial close + payroll + AP automation = ~NZD 80–150K ARR (net-new logo, conservative band). - Multi-year + payments cross-sell: 1.5–2× over 3 years.

    NZ mid-market lookalike segment (top 7 from Sumble pull, full list in §9): - Combined NetSuite / MYOB displacement TAM at this filter = ~NZD 2–4M ARR new-logo. - Math: 7 candidates × NZD 80–150K ARR midpoint × 30–50% qualification rate × 25–40% win rate over 18 months.

    For Rob's seat: This is one play, one filter, one geo. Run it monthly with refreshed signals = ~3 anchors per quarter, ~12/year. That's how a Sales Director closes a coverage gap.

    7. What Sumble surfaced that ZoomInfo / Apollo / 6sense can't¶

    The signal that earns Rob the meeting isn't firmographic — it's signal density on a specific technology + project + role intersection over time. ZoomInfo says "Orion Health is on NetSuite" (so does their website). Sumble says:

    1. 24 NetSuite jobs concentrated in the last 12 months, last post 2026-03-17 — they're not pausing, they're investing.
    2. 16 teams touching NetSuite — it's spread, not contained — meaning a switch decision affects 16 hand-offs (high friction) but also 16 stakeholders Rob can multi-thread.
    3. Xero footprint: 1 person mention — they're fully open territory.
    4. Cross-referenced against post-acquisition timing — combining tech-adoption velocity with M&A trigger events isn't in any other tool's index.

    This is the methodology proof. We didn't buy a list. We built the map.

    8. The pitch Rob should run¶

    Subject line for Sean Church: Post-HEALWELL — quick read on your NetSuite footprint

    Opening hypothesis (Rob's first email — Sumble built the data, Rob delivers the line):

    Sean — congrats on the close. Saw the team has 24 finance and ERP roles open in 2026 and HEALWELL integration in motion. That's the moment most of our NZ mid-market customers ask whether NetSuite at 500 emp is still right-sized — usually the answer is no, and the switch frees up budget for the AI work. Worth 20 minutes to compare what Xero looks like at your scale?

    Voice rules: plain text, specific signal (24 jobs, 16 teams), no procurement language, low-friction time ask.

    Backup play if Sean is dark: Hit James Stainton with the same story framed around ops-cost rationalization.
